# AI Supply Chain Trust — Logo Exploration Brief

## Strategic findings

- The software supply-chain security category is visually saturated with shields, padlocks, chain links, hexagons, and standalone check marks.
- The product's defensible idea is not generic “security”; it is evidence-backed provenance: tracing a repository through live signals, scoring eight evidence pillars, and producing a decision.
- The existing interface is editorial and restrained: black/white surfaces, a single evidence-green accent, compact geometry, and monospaced data labels.
- The mark must therefore communicate a verified path or evidence convergence without illustrating a literal shield or chain.

## Shared production constraints

- Brand: `AI Supply Chain Trust`
- Use case: `logo-brand`
- Produce one standalone symbol only; no wordmark during concept generation.
- Flat vector-friendly geometry on a plain white square canvas.
- Maximum five major shapes and one intentional negative-space gesture.
- Must survive at 16 px, work in one color, and have a memorable outer silhouette.
- Use near-black `#0B1117` with evidence green `#16B978` only.
- No gradients, shadows, bevels, 3D, thin hairlines, mockups, watermarks, or decorative background.
- Explicitly avoid shields, locks, check marks, chain-link icons, cubes, generic hexagons, circuit brains, eyes, fingerprints, GitHub/Octocat imagery, and literal letters.

## Direction A — Provenance Gate (Recraft V4.1 Pro Vector)

Create a rigorous geometric mark from two offset, open rectangular frames. A single diagonal evidence path enters at lower left, passes through both frames, and exits at upper right. The frames should feel like successive verification gates; the central negative space should form a small, unmistakable diamond “proof point.” Use only four or five bold shapes, with optical corrections and consistent stroke mass. The silhouette must feel calm, institutional, and ownable—not like a fintech app or a logistics company.

## Direction B — Evidence Fold (OpenAI GPT Image 2)

Create an abstract folded ribbon made of three broad orthogonal segments representing source, build, and artifact. The ribbon changes direction twice and resolves into a stable central void, suggesting traceable provenance becoming trustworthy. The void may subtly imply a lowercase `t` through geometry, but must not become a literal letter. Prefer a compact asymmetric form with tension and excellent negative space, similar in discipline to a top-tier infrastructure brand.

## Direction C — Trust Aperture (Google Gemini 3.1 Flash Image)

Create a compact aperture-like mark from four thick corner brackets that progressively align toward one off-center proof point. One bracket is evidence green and completes the system. The motion should read as independent evidence converging into a verified decision. Avoid camera-aperture resemblance, flowers, stars, and medical crosses. Keep the outer contour almost square but with a distinctive missing corner.

## Direction D — Lineage Relay (Black Forest Labs FLUX.2 Pro)

Create a bold, minimal mark showing a continuous provenance relay: three solid modules arranged along a rising stepped path, connected by one uninterrupted negative-space channel. The first and second modules are near-black; the final module is evidence green, indicating verified output. The negative channel—not an added arrow—should create forward motion. Keep the silhouette compact and unexpected, avoiding bar charts, stair icons, package boxes, arrows, and blockchain clichés.

## Final concept — Provenance Channel

The final mark is built around the negative space rather than a security cliché. A single uninterrupted white channel moves through two dark evidence stages and terminates at a smaller green verified artifact. The overall motion loosely recalls an `S` for supply chain without becoming a literal monogram. The mark contains no shield, lock, chain link, arrow, package, or check mark.
